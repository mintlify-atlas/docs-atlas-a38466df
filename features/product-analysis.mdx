## Overview

The **Product Analysis** view provides comprehensive insights into your product portfolio performance. From category-level trends to individual SKU analysis, this dashboard helps identify bestsellers, slow movers, and growth opportunities across your catalog.

<Info>
  **Who uses this view**: Product managers, commercial analysts, category managers, and sales leaders responsible for portfolio optimization and inventory planning.
</Info>

## Product Portfolio Dashboard

Four key metrics summarize your product performance:

### Product Pareto Chart

The **Product Pareto Analysis** applies the 80/20 rule to your SKU portfolio:

**Chart components**:
- **Bars**: Individual product sales (top 30 shown, sorted DESC)
- **Line**: Cumulative percentage of total revenue
- **80% marker**: Shows which products generate 80% of sales

**What this tells you**:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Category A Products" icon="star" color="#00C49F">
    Products before the 80% line:
    - Your revenue drivers (typically 20% of SKUs)
    - Never allow stockouts
    - Focus promotions here
    - Train all sellers on these items
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Category B Products" icon="chart-simple" color="#FFB347">
    Products from 80% to 95%:
    - Supporting cast (typically 30% of SKUs)
    - Maintain adequate stock
    - Occasional promotions
    - Standard training coverage
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Category C Products" icon="boxes-stacked" color="#6C9EE5">
    Products after 95% line:
    - Long tail (typically 50% of SKUs)
    - Consider discontinuation candidates
    - Minimal inventory investment
    - Made-to-order if possible
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  If your 80% line occurs at fewer than 10-15 products, you have dangerous product concentration. Diversification should be a strategic priority.
</Note>

## Products Without Movement

The **Stagnant Products (60 Days)** panel highlights SKUs requiring attention:

### Detection Criteria

<Warning>
  A product is flagged as "stagnant" if:
  - Product status = Active (not discontinued)
  - AND zero sales in the last 60 days
  - AND available in inventory
</Warning>

### Why This Matters

**Business impact**:
- Inventory carrying costs
- Warehouse space waste
- Cash flow tied up
- Risk of expiration/obsolescence
- Opportunity cost (could stock faster movers)

### Action Playbook

**For each stagnant product, investigate**:

1. **Availability Issues**
   - Is it actually in stock?
   - Distribution gaps?
   - Logistics problems?

2. **Sales Team Knowledge**
   - Do sellers know this product exists?
   - Training gap?
   - Missing from sales materials?

3. **Pricing Problems**
   - Uncompetitive pricing?
   - Better alternatives available?
   - Wrong price tier?

4. **Market Conditions**
   - Seasonal product out of season?
   - Consumer preference shift?
   - Competitive disruption?

5. **Product Lifecycle**
   - End of life product?
   - Replacement available?
   - Discontinuation candidate?

### Intervention Options

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Promotional Push" icon="badge-percent">
    - Discount to move inventory
    - Bundle with popular items
    - Limited-time offer
    - Featured in weekly specials
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Training Campaign" icon="chalkboard-user">
    - Product knowledge session
    - Sales team incentive
    - Demo units for sellers
    - Benefits/features refresher
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Pricing Adjustment" icon="dollar-sign">
    - Market comparison analysis
    - Competitive pricing research
    - Margin vs volume trade-off
    - Strategic repositioning
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Discontinuation" icon="ban">
    - Mark as inactive
    - Liquidate remaining stock
    - Replace with better alternative
    - Free up catalog space
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  Best practice: Review stagnant products monthly. Products inactive 90+ days are strong discontinuation candidates unless seasonal.
</Note>

## High-Growth Products

The **Products with Highest Growth** panel identifies rising stars:

### Growth Detection

<Info>
  A product is flagged as "high growth" if:
  - Revenue vs previous period > +10%
  - AND minimum volume threshold met (filters noise)
  - Sorted by growth percentage (highest first)
</Info>

### Growth Analysis

**Understand why products are growing**:

1. **Market Trends**
   - Riding consumer trend wave?
   - Seasonal surge?
   - Category expansion?

2. **Your Actions**
   - Recent promotion impact?
   - Price reduction effect?
   - Improved availability?

3. **Competitive Factors**
   - Competitor stockout?
   - Better than alternatives?
   - Exclusive offering?

4. **Product Quality**
   - Superior features?
   - Better value proposition?
   - Positive word-of-mouth?

## Workflow Examples

### Weekly Product Review (15 min)

1. **Check Stagnant Products panel**
   - Any new products without movement?
   - Follow up on last week's action items

2. **Scan Growth Products**
   - Identify surprise performers
   - Ensure adequate stock levels

3. **Review Top 10 Products**
   - Any unusual changes?
   - Stock availability concerns?

### Monthly Category Review (1 hour)

1. **Select each category individually**
2. **Analyze category Pareto chart**
3. **Identify category A products** (protect at all costs)
4. **Review category C products** (discontinuation candidates)
5. **Check category growth trends**
6. **Update category strategy**

### Quarterly Portfolio Optimization (4 hours)

1. **Generate full product ranking**
2. **Identify discontinuation candidates**:
   - Category C products
   - Stagnant 90+ days
   - Low margin
   - Supplier issues
3. **Create new product introduction plan**
4. **Reallocate inventory investment**
5. **Update sales team training priorities**
6. **Set product-specific targets for next quarter**

## Data Calculations

### Active SKUs Count
```
Count of distinct id_producto
where activo = True
in productos master table
```

### Stagnant Products Detection
```
For each product in productos where activo = True:
  last_sale_date = MAX(fecha) where id_producto = product
  if last_sale_date < (today - 60 days) OR last_sale_date is NULL:
    flag as stagnant
```

### Average Sales per SKU
```
Total Revenue / Count(distinct id_producto with sales > 0)
```

### Pareto Classification
```
1. Sort all products by revenue DESC
2. Calculate cumulative % of total revenue
3. Assign:
   - Category A: Products until 80% cumulative
   - Category B: Products from 80% to 95%
   - Category C: Products from 95% to 100%
```

### Growth Calculation
```
growth_pct = (revenue_current_period - revenue_previous_period) / 
             revenue_previous_period
```
