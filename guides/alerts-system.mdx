## Overview

Visor KPI's **Alert Engine** continuously monitors your commercial data and automatically identifies:

- 🔴 **Critical Issues**: Performance problems requiring immediate action
- 🟡 **Medium Risks**: Potential problems to monitor closely
- 🔵 **Low Priority**: Minor issues for awareness
- 🟢 **Opportunities**: Positive signals for growth

The alert engine evaluates **7 distinct rules** against your filtered data every time you load the dashboard.

<Info>
**Real-Time Evaluation**

Alerts are calculated dynamically based on your current filter selections (date range, zone, salesperson, channel). Change filters to see different alert sets.
</Info>

## Alert Rules

All 7 alert rules are defined in `src/alerts_engine.py:14-71`.

### ALT001: Salesperson Low Performance

<Card title="ALT001: Vendedor bajo rendimiento crítico" icon="user-xmark" color="#E84855">
  **Severity**: 🔴 Critical (alta)
  
  **Trigger Condition**:
  ```python
  cumplimiento_pct < 0.65 in últimos 30 días
  ```
  
  Salesperson has achieved **less than 65%** of their target in the last 30 days.
  
  **Entity Type**: `vendedor`
  
  **Suggested Action**: "Revisar cartera y objetivos con supervisor"
  
  **Explanation**: This is the most critical salesperson alert. When someone is below 65%, they're significantly off-target and need immediate intervention.
  
  **Detection Logic** (`alerts_engine.py:102-124`):
  1. Filter sales to last 30 days from `fecha_hasta`
  2. Sum sales per salesperson
  3. Get monthly target from `Objetivos` sheet
  4. Calculate: `cumplimiento = ventas_30d / objetivo_mes`
  5. Alert if `cumplimiento < 0.65`
  
  **Next Steps**:
  - Review salesperson's customer portfolio
  - Check for systemic issues (territory problems, resource gaps)
  - Reassess target if unrealistic
  - Provide coaching or additional support
</Card>

### ALT002: Customer Inactivity

<Card title="ALT002: Cliente sin compras (riesgo de pérdida)" icon="building-circle-xmark" color="#FFB347">
  **Severity**: 🟡 Medium (media)
  
  **Trigger Condition**:
  ```python
  cliente activo sin compras en los últimos 45 días
  ```
  
  Active customer (from `Clientes` sheet with `activo = TRUE`) has **not made a purchase in 45+ days**.
  
  **Entity Type**: `cliente`
  
  **Suggested Action**: "Contacto de recuperación urgente"
  
  **Explanation**: Dormant customers are at high risk of churning to competitors. 45 days is the threshold for intervention.
  
  **Detection Logic** (`alerts_engine.py:126-145`):
  1. Get last purchase date per customer: `ultima_compra = df.groupby("id_cliente")["fecha"].max()`
  2. Calculate days since: `dias = (fecha_hasta - ultima_compra).days`
  3. Alert if customer is active AND (`ultima_compra` is `None` OR `dias > 45`)
  
  **Next Steps**:
  - Call or visit the customer immediately
  - Identify reason for inactivity (competitor, service issue, changing needs)
  - Offer promotion or incentive to reactivate
  - Assign to salesperson if unassigned
</Card>

### ALT003: Category A Customer Drop

<Card title="ALT003: Caída de venta en cliente relevante" icon="chart-line-down" color="#E84855">
  **Severity**: 🔴 Critical (alta)
  
  **Trigger Condition**:
  ```python
  cliente categoría A con caída >25% vs mes anterior
  ```
  
  A **Pareto Category A customer** (top 20% by revenue) has experienced a **>25% revenue drop** compared to the previous period.
  
  **Entity Type**: `cliente`
  
  **Suggested Action**: "Visita de cuenta clave"
  
  **Explanation**: Category A customers are your most valuable accounts. A significant drop requires immediate executive attention.
  
  **Detection Logic** (`alerts_engine.py:147-175`):
  1. Calculate Pareto categories for current period customers
  2. Identify Category A customers
  3. Compare current period revenue vs. previous period
  4. Alert if: `(revenue_current - revenue_previous) / revenue_previous < -0.25`
  
  **Detail Display**:
  ```
  Caída: -32.4% ($450,000 → $304,200)
  ```
  
  **Next Steps**:
  - Schedule immediate visit with senior management
  - Investigate root cause (competitor offer, service failure, budget cuts)
  - Prepare recovery plan with incentives
  - Escalate internally if relationship at risk
</Card>

### ALT004: Product No Movement

<Card title="ALT004: Producto sin movimiento" icon="box" color="#4A90E2">
  **Severity**: 🔵 Low (baja)
  
  **Trigger Condition**:
  ```python
  producto activo sin ventas en 60 días
  ```
  
  A product marked as active (`activo = TRUE` in `Productos` sheet) has had **no sales in 60+ days**.
  
  **Entity Type**: `producto`
  
  **Suggested Action**: "Revisar disponibilidad y push comercial"
  
  **Explanation**: Slow-moving inventory ties up capital and may indicate availability issues, lack of awareness, or market shift.
  
  **Detection Logic** (`alerts_engine.py:177-197`):
  1. Get last sale date per product: `ultima_venta = df.groupby("id_producto")["fecha"].max()`
  2. Filter to active products: `productos_df[activo == True]`
  3. Alert if `ultima_venta` is `None` OR `dias_sin_venta > 60`
  
  **Next Steps**:
  - Verify stock availability
  - Check if product is featured in sales materials
  - Consider promotion or bundle with faster-moving products
  - Review pricing vs. competition
  - If persistently slow, consider discontinuing
</Card>

### ALT005: Salesperson Inactivity

<Card title="ALT005: Vendedor sin actividad reciente" icon="user-clock" color="#FFB347">
  **Severity**: 🟡 Medium (media)
  
  **Trigger Condition**:
  ```python
  vendedor sin transacciones en los últimos 7 días hábiles
  ```
  
  Salesperson has recorded **no transactions in the last 7 business days**.
  
  **Entity Type**: `vendedor`
  
  **Suggested Action**: "Verificar actividad de campo"
  
  **Explanation**: Extended periods without activity may indicate illness, vacation, territory issues, or disengagement.
  
  **Detection Logic** (`alerts_engine.py:199-217`):
  1. Calculate 7 business days ago: `_retroceder_dias_habiles(fecha_hasta, 7)`
  2. Get last transaction date per salesperson
  3. Alert if `ultima_actividad < cutoff_date`
  
  **Business Days Calculation** (`alerts_engine.py:342-350`):
  ```python
  def _retroceder_dias_habiles(hoy: date, n: int) -> date:
      d = hoy
      count = 0
      while count < n:
          d -= timedelta(days=1)
          if d.weekday() < 5:  # Monday=0, Friday=4
              count += 1
      return d
  ```
  
  **Next Steps**:
  - Contact salesperson to verify status
  - Check if on approved leave
  - Review territory for systemic issues
  - Reassign critical accounts if prolonged absence
</Card>

  **Next Steps**:

</Card>

### ALT007: Team Near Monthly Target

<Card title="ALT007: Equipo cerca del objetivo mensual" icon="bullseye" color="#00C49F">
  **Severity**: 🟢 Opportunity (info)
  
  **Trigger Condition**:
  ```python
  cumplimiento general >85% y quedan más de 5 días hábiles
  ```
  
  The team has achieved **>85% of monthly target** and there are **more than 5 business days remaining** in the month.
  
  **Entity Type**: `equipo`
  
  **Suggested Action**: "Push final para cerrar mes en verde"
  
  **Explanation**: This motivational alert identifies when the team is close to target with enough time to close the gap.
  
  **Detection Logic** (`alerts_engine.py:249-270`):
  1. Sum all monthly targets: `objetivo_total = objetivos_df[periodo.month == current_month]["objetivo"].sum()`
  2. Sum all current month sales: `ventas_mes = df[fecha.month == current_month]["importe_neto"].sum()`
  3. Calculate achievement: `cumplimiento = ventas_mes / objetivo_total`
  4. Count business days remaining: `_dias_habiles_restantes_mes(fecha_hasta)`
  5. Alert if `cumplimiento > 0.85 AND dias_restantes > 5`
  
  **Business Days Remaining** (`alerts_engine.py:353-361`):
  ```python
  def _dias_habiles_restantes_mes(hoy: date) -> int:
      from calendar import monthrange
      ultimo_dia = monthrange(hoy.year, hoy.month)[1]
      count = 0
      for d in range(hoy.day + 1, ultimo_dia + 1):
          if date(hoy.year, hoy.month, d).weekday() < 5:
              count += 1
      return count
  ```
  
  **Next Steps**:
  - Send motivational communication to team
  - Offer short-term incentives (bonus, recognition)
  - Focus on closing pipeline deals
  - Accelerate decision-making on pending quotes
</Card>

### Mark as Reviewed

Implementation (`alerts_engine.py:196-202`):

```python
if st.button("✅ Marcar revisada", key=f"_rev_{alerta_key}"):
    st.session_state["alertas_revisadas"].add(alerta_key)
    st.rerun()
```

**Alert Key Format**: `{id_alerta}_{entidad_id}`

Example: `ALT001_VEND_0012`

<Warning>
**Session-Based Tracking**

Reviewed status is stored in `st.session_state["alertas_revisadas"]` (a Python `set`). This is **not persisted** to disk.

Reviewed alerts reset when:
- You close the browser
- Session times out
- App restarts

For persistent alert tracking, you would need to add database storage.
</Warning>

## Alert Data Structure

### DataFrame Columns

The `get_alertas_activas()` function returns a DataFrame with these columns:

| Column | Type | Description |
|--------|------|-------------|
| `id_alerta` | str | Rule ID (ALT001-ALT007) |
| `nombre` | str | Alert name/title |
| `severidad` | str | "alta", "media", "baja", "info" |
| `entidad_tipo` | str | "vendedor", "cliente", "producto", "equipo" |
| `entidad_id` | str | Unique ID of affected entity |
| `entidad_nombre` | str | Display name of entity |
| `valor_actual` | float | Current metric value |
| `valor_referencia` | float | Threshold/benchmark value |
| `descripcion_detalle` | str | Human-readable detail message |
| `fecha_deteccion` | date | Date alert was detected |
| `accion_sugerida` | str | Recommended action |

### Sorting

Alerts are sorted by severity using this order:

```python
orden_sev = {"alta": 0, "media": 1, "baja": 2, "info": 3}
```

Critical alerts always appear first.

## Customizing Alerts

### Modifying Thresholds

To adjust alert sensitivity, edit values in `alerts_engine.py:14-71`:

**Example: Change ALT001 threshold from 65% to 70%**:

```python
{
    "id":              "ALT001",
    "nombre":          "Vendedor bajo rendimiento crítico",
    "condicion":       "cumplimiento_pct < 0.70 en los últimos 30 días",  # Changed
    "severidad":       "alta",
    ...
}
```

Then update the detection logic:

```python
if cumpl < 0.70:  # Changed from 0.65
    alertas.append(...)
```

### Adding New Alert Rules

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define Rule">
    Add to `ALERT_RULES` list:
    ```python
    {
        "id":              "ALT008",
        "nombre":          "Your Alert Name",
        "condicion":       "Description of trigger condition",
        "severidad":       "alta"|"media"|"baja"|"info",
        "entidad_tipo":    "vendedor"|"cliente"|"producto"|"equipo",
        "accion_sugerida": "What to do about it",
    }
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Implement Detection Logic">
    Add detection code in `get_alertas_activas()` function:
    ```python
    # ── ALT008: Your Custom Alert ──────────────────────────
    if condition_is_met:
        alertas.append(_crear_alerta(
            regla       = ALERT_RULES[7],  # Index of new rule
            entidad_id  = entity_id,
            entidad_nom = entity_name,
            val_actual  = current_value,
            val_ref     = threshold_value,
            detalle     = f"Detail message: {value}",
            fecha       = fecha_hasta,
        ))
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Test">
    1. Restart Streamlit app
    2. Navigate to Alert Center
    3. Verify new alert appears when conditions are met
    4. Test with different filter combinations
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Changing Severity Levels

You can reclassify any alert's severity by changing its `"severidad"` value:

- `"alta"` (🔴) - Critical, immediate action required
- `"media"` (🟡) - Medium priority, monitor closely
- `"baja"` (🔵) - Low priority, for awareness
- `"info"` (🟢) - Positive opportunity, non-urgent

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="No Alerts Showing" icon="bell-slash">
    **Possible Causes**:
    1. No data in selected period/filters
    2. All metrics are healthy (good news!)
    3. Thresholds are too strict
    
    **Diagnosis**:
    - Check "Resumen Ejecutivo" to verify data is loading
    - Clear all filters to see if alerts appear
    - Review alert thresholds in `alerts_engine.py`
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Too Many Alerts" icon="bell-exclamation">
    **Possible Causes**:
    1. Thresholds too sensitive
    2. Systemic performance issues
    3. Data quality problems
    
    **Solutions**:
    - Adjust thresholds to focus on most critical issues
    - Use severity filters to prioritize
    - Investigate root causes (may indicate broader business problems)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Alert Doesn't Make Sense" icon="question">
    **Possible Causes**:
    1. Bug in detection logic
    2. Data quality issue (wrong joins, missing data)
    3. Misunderstanding of metric definition
    
    **Diagnosis**:
    - Check `valor_actual` vs `valor_referencia` in alert detail
    - Verify entity exists in raw data
    - Review KPI calculation in `kpi_engine.py`
    - Check for NULL values or data type mismatches
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Reviewed Status Not Saving" icon="floppy-disk-circle-xmark">
    **Expected Behavior**: Reviewed status is **session-based** and does not persist across browser sessions or app restarts.
    
    If you need persistent tracking:
    1. Add a database table for alert tracking
    2. Store reviewed status with user ID and timestamp
    3. Query on load to populate `st.session_state["alertas_revisadas"]`
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Technical Reference

### Main Function Signature

```python
def get_alertas_activas(
    df: pd.DataFrame,
    df_anterior: pd.DataFrame | None,
    clientes_df: pd.DataFrame,
    productos_df: pd.DataFrame,
    objetivos_df: pd.DataFrame | None = None,
    fecha_hasta: date | None = None,
) -> pd.DataFrame:
```

**Parameters**:
- `df` - Current period filtered sales data
- `df_anterior` - Previous period data for comparisons
- `clientes_df` - Customer master data
- `productos_df` - Product master data
- `objetivos_df` - Target/objective data
- `fecha_hasta` - Reference date (usually filter end date)

**Returns**: DataFrame with columns listed in [Alert Data Structure](#alert-data-structure)

### Helper Functions

- `_crear_alerta()` - Constructs alert dict from rule + data
- `_get_nombre_vendedor()` - Gets salesperson display name
- `_get_nombre_cliente()` - Gets customer display name  
- `_get_objetivo_mes()` - Gets monthly target by salesperson
- `_retroceder_dias_habiles()` - Calculates N business days ago
- `_dias_habiles_restantes_mes()` - Counts business days left in month

**Source**: `alerts_engine.py:292-361`
