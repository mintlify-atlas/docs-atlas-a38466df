### Pareto Chart

The **Client Pareto Chart** visualizes revenue concentration:

- **Bar chart**: Individual client contributions (top 30 shown)
- **Cumulative line**: Running percentage of total revenue
- **80% marker**: Shows where Category A ends

**How to read it**:
- X-axis: Clients ranked by revenue (highest to lowest)
- Left Y-axis: Revenue contribution
- Right Y-axis: Cumulative percentage
- The steeper the line, the more concentrated your portfolio

<Warning>
  If your line reaches 80% in fewer than 10 clients, you have dangerous concentration risk. One lost account could severely impact revenue.
</Warning>

## Client Segmentation Scatter

The **Purchase Frequency vs Revenue** scatter plot reveals hidden patterns:

### Four Quadrants

```
        High Revenue
            │
   Champions │ Potentials
─────────────┼─────────────
   Occasional│ Small Buyers
            │
        Low Revenue
```

**Quadrant interpretations**:

1. **Champions** (High Frequency + High Revenue)
   - Your best clients—protect at all costs
   - Category A accounts
   - Action: White-glove service

2. **Potentials** (Low Frequency + High Revenue)
   - Large orders but infrequent
   - Category A/B accounts
   - Action: Increase visit frequency

3. **Occasional** (High Frequency + Low Revenue)
   - Loyal but small transactions
   - Category B/C accounts
   - Action: Upselling opportunity

4. **Small Buyers** (Low Frequency + Low Revenue)
   - Minimal engagement
   - Category C accounts
   - Action: Automation or re-evaluation

<Note>
  Each dot represents a client. Hover over any point to see client name, exact purchase count, and total revenue.
</Note>

## Concentration Metrics

Three statistical indicators measure portfolio risk:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Category A Clients" icon="users">
    - **Count**: Number of A-class clients
    - **% of Portfolio**: e.g., "18 clients (20%)" 
    - Lower % is typical (Pareto law)
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Category A Revenue" icon="dollar-sign">
    - **% of Total Sales**: e.g., "78.5%"
    - **Target**: 75-85% is healthy
    - Below 70% = too fragmented
    - Above 90% = too concentrated
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Gini Index" icon="chart-line">
    - **Value**: 0.000 to 1.000
    - **0 = perfect equality** (all clients equal)
    - **1 = total concentration** (one client = all revenue)
    - **Healthy range**: 0.45 - 0.65
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Gini Index Interpretation

| Gini Score | Portfolio Health | Action Required |
|------------|------------------|------------------|
| < 0.40 | Too fragmented | Focus on key accounts |
| 0.40 - 0.65 | ✅ Balanced | Maintain current strategy |
| 0.65 - 0.75 | ⚠️ High concentration | Diversification plan |
| > 0.75 | 🔴 Dangerous concentration | Urgent diversification |

<Warning>
  A Gini index above 0.70 means you're dependent on a small number of clients. Losing just one could be catastrophic. Prioritize new business development immediately.
</Warning>

## At-Risk Clients

The **Clients at Risk** panel highlights accounts in danger of churn:

### Risk Detection Rules

<Warning>
  A client is flagged at-risk if:
  - Pareto Category = A or B (valuable account)
  - AND days since last purchase > 30
  - AND purchase frequency declining
</Warning>

### Risk Levels

| Days Inactive | Risk Level | Action Priority |
|---------------|------------|------------------|
| 30-45 days | 🟡 Medium | Schedule call this week |
| 45-60 days | 🟠 High | In-person visit required |
| 60+ days | 🔴 Critical | Manager escalation |

### Intervention Playbook

**For at-risk clients**:

1. **Research Phase** (Day 30)
   - Review recent order history
   - Check for price disputes or quality issues
   - Talk to delivery/logistics team

2. **Contact Phase** (Day 35-40)
   - Personal phone call from account manager
   - Ask open-ended questions about needs
   - Offer special promotion or incentive

3. **Escalation Phase** (Day 45+)
   - In-person visit with manager
   - Custom retention proposal
   - Address any service gaps

4. **Win-Back Phase** (Day 60+)
   - Executive-level engagement
   - Significant pricing concession if needed
   - Long-term contract negotiation

<Note>
  Prevention is cheaper than reactivation. Category A clients should never reach 60+ days inactive—catch them early.
</Note>

## Workflow Examples

### Weekly Portfolio Review (15 min)

1. Check **At-Risk Clients** panel
2. Sort by days inactive
3. Create call list for top 10
4. Note specific issues for each
5. Execute calls throughout week

### Monthly Strategic Planning (45 min)

1. Review **Pareto Chart** for concentration risk
2. Check **Gini Index** trend
3. Analyze **Growth Opportunities** list
4. Identify 3-5 accounts for intensive focus
5. Create account development plans
6. Allocate resources accordingly

### Quarterly Business Review (2 hours)

1. Filter to **Category A** clients only
2. Review each account's 6-month trend
3. Flag any declining accounts
4. Schedule face-to-face reviews
5. Prepare custom retention offers
6. Update account plans in CRM

## Data Calculations

### Pareto Classification Algorithm

```
1. Sort all clients by revenue DESC
2. Calculate cumulative % of total revenue
3. Assign categories:
   - Category A: Clients until cumulative reaches 80%
   - Category B: Clients from 80% to 95%
   - Category C: Remaining clients (95-100%)
```

### Gini Index Formula

```
Gini = (Sum of absolute differences in revenue) / 
       (2 × n × sum of all revenues)

where n = number of clients
```

### Client Risk Score

```
if category in ['A', 'B']:
    if days_since_last_purchase > 30:
        risk = True
    if vs_previous_period < -0.25:
        risk = Critical
```
